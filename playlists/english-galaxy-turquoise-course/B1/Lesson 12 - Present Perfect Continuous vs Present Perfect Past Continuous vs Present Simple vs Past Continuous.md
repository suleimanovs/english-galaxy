## Past Continuous

- When I <font color="#10b981">**was going**</font> home, she <font color="#10b981">**was waiting**</font> for me. — два действия в прошлом одновременно.
- She <font color="#10b981">**was staying**</font> at work all day. — **all day** в прошлом времени указывает на Past Continuous.
- He <font color="#10b981">**was learning**</font> English all day yesterday. — **all day** в прошлом времени указывает на Past Continuous.

## Past Simple

> [!note] Лексика
> **potential threat** — потенциальная угроза.

- She <font color="#10b981">**decided**</font> to make a career, so she <font color="#10b981">**continued**</font> working there.
- They <font color="#10b981">**knew**</font> that it was a very serious potential threat.

## Present Perfect

- I <font color="#10b981">**have never been**</font> to this place.

## Present Perfect Continuous

- I <font color="#10b981">**have been waiting**</font> for it all my life.
- She <font color="#10b981">**has been learning**</font> English all day.
- How long <font color="#10b981">**have**</font> you <font color="#10b981">**been waiting**</font>?

## Past Perfect

- She said that they <font color="#10b981">**had known**</font> each other for a very long time.
- She told me that she <font color="#10b981">**had never thought**</font> about it seriously.
- She told me that it <font color="#10b981">**hadn't happened**</font> yet.

## Past Perfect Continuous

- When I saw him, she <font color="#10b981">**had already been waiting**</font> for her for twenty minutes.
- He said that he <font color="#10b981">**had been waiting**</font> for it for a long time.

## Смешанная практика

- She told me that she **had been waiting** for it for a very long time. — Past Perfect Continuous.
- When I called him, he **was driving** a car.
- When I came there, I noticed his absence.
- He said that he **had already been** there.
- He **was typing** a message when he heard a call.
- When they met, they decided to do it immediately.
- He said that he **had managed** to overcome all these difficulties on his way.
- He **had finished** watching this movie by six.
- I was very happy to hear that I **had won**.
- I **had reached** this level by last year.
- What **were** you **doing** when it happened?
- To be honest, it **has never interested** me.
- To be honest, I **have never been interested** in it.
- I said that I **had never been interested** in it.
- I told him that I **had never been interested** in it.
